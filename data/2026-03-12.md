Motivation: 系统研究大质量恒星从中微子发射到早期超新星阶段的演化过程，探索恒星内部结构与中微子发射特征之间的物理关联

Method: 使用前身星和超新星模型，分析10-40太阳质量恒星的中微子光度和能谱，研究致密参数和碳氧核心质量与中微子发射的相关性

Result: 发现致密参数和碳氧核心质量与中微子发射强相关；超新星早期阶段中微子性质对恒星演化代码不敏感；中微子发射特征反映前身星致密性；可通过联合分析两个阶段获得互补约束

Conclusion: 系统研究中微子发射为理解大质量恒星内部结构提供新途径，联合分析前超新星和早期超新星阶段可获得互补约束，数据公开促进进一步研究

Abstract: We present the first systematic study of neutrino emissions from massive stars, continuously tracking the late evolutionary stages through the early core-collapse supernova phase. Using progenitor and supernova models, we analyze the neutrino luminosities and spectra for progenitors with initial masses of 10--40~$M_\odot$. Our systematic analysis reveals that the compactness parameter ($ξ_{2.5}$) and carbon-oxygen core mass ($M_{\text{CO}}$) exhibit strong correlations with neutrino emission. In the pre-supernova phase, the time-integrated number of neutrinos correlates with $ξ_{2.5}$ when integrated over the final day and with $M_{\text{CO}}$ for longer durations. For the early supernova phase ($<200$ ms post-bounce), the neutrino properties are relatively insensitive to the specific stellar evolution code used, allowing for a reliable extraction of physical correlations. We confirm that the neutrino emission features, including the electron neutrino burst properties and accretion-powered luminosity of other species, reflect the progenitor's compactness. An evaluation of the observational feasibility for a nearby progenitor using a False Alarm Rate approach suggests that these correlations can persist even under practical detection conditions. Such a joint analysis of both phases provides complementary constraints on the internal structure. All calculated time-series data will be made publicly available.
