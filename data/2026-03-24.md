### [1] [Early emission characterization of TDE2025aarm](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.20123)
*Andrea Simongini,Maria Kherlakian,Alicia López-Oramas,Josefa Becerra*

Main category: astro-ph.HE

TL;DR: TDE2025aarm是第二近的潮汐瓦解事件，通过多波段观测发现其早期光学光谱呈蓝连续谱并含氦、氢线，光变曲线可用0.16太阳质量恒星落入2×10⁷太阳质量黑洞的回落过程描述，X射线探测表明其为光学/UV亮且软X射线探测的新TDE类型。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 研究TDE2025aarm这一第二近的潮汐瓦解事件，因其近距离提供了前所未有的详细研究机会，旨在通过多波段观测理解其早期辐射特性和物理机制。

Method: 使用利物浦望远镜进行三期光学观测，结合辅助光谱和测光数据，以及Swift-XRT的X射线观测（0.3-10 keV波段），通过光谱分析、光变曲线拟合和X射线谱拟合进行研究。

Result: 光学光谱显示蓝连续谱和氦、氢及可能的鲍文线；光变曲线峰值Mg ~ -18.63星等，可用0.16太阳质量恒星落入2×10⁷太阳质量黑洞的回落过程描述；X射线探测到总通量~1.42×10⁻¹⁴ erg s⁻¹ cm⁻²，黑体拟合温度~0.39 keV。

Conclusion: TDE2025aarm是光学/UV亮且软X射线探测的新TDE类型，其早期辐射由环化激波提供能量，延迟吸积场景最能解释观测特征。

Abstract: In this Letter, we present early emission data analysis of the tidal disruption event TDE2025aarm, including optical, UV and X-ray data.
  At a redshift of z = 0.01368, TDE2025aarm is the second closest TDE ever discovered, offering an unprecedented opportunity to study such phenomena in great details.
  We observed TDE2025aarm in optical with the Liverpool Telescope for a total of three epochs, and complemented our dataset with ancillary spectroscopic and photometric data.
  The early optical spectra are characterized by a blue-continuum and helium, hydrogen and possibly Bowen lines typical of H+He events.
  The optical light curves peak at M_g ~ -18.63 mag and are well described by fallback of a M_star ~ 0.16 M_sun star onto a M_BH ~ 2x10^{7} M_sun black hole.
  We report Swift-XRT detection in the 0.3-10 keV range, with a total flux of F_X ~ 1.42x10^{-14} erg s-1 cm-2, fitted by a black-body with kT ~ 0.39 keV.
  This makes TDE2025aarm a new event among optical/UV bright TDEs detected in soft X-rays.
  Our analysis suggests that the early emission from TDE2025aarm is powered by circularization shocks, and that the delayed accretion scenario best describes the observed features.

### [2] [Numerically stable equations for the orbital evolution of compact object binaries](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.20124)
*Max M. Briel,Jeff J. Andrews*

Main category: astro-ph.HE

TL;DR: 提出一种在ln空间重写Peters方程的数值方法，使标准数值求解器能够收敛，提高计算效率和鲁棒性


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: Peters和Mathews推导的引力波致密双星轨道演化方程在引力波领域广泛应用，但缺乏闭合解，需要数值积分。现有数值方法在接近并合点时容易失效，而轨道演化问题未得到足够关注

Method: 将Peters方程在ln空间重写，使常用数值求解器能够收敛，提供更稳健的数值方法

Result: 新方法显著提高了数值鲁棒性和计算效率，在测试中将函数评估次数减少了60%到70%

Conclusion: ln空间重写的Peters方程为引力波致密双星轨道演化提供了更高效、更稳健的数值计算方法

Abstract: The orbital and eccentricity evolution for compact object binaries through gravitational wave emission first derived by Peters and Mathews are used extensively throughout the gravitational wave community for calculating the orbital evolution and merger time of compact binaries. While improved calculations of the binary merger time have been the focus of several investigations since, the orbital evolution has not received the same attention. As the equations lack a closed form solution, a numerical integrator is required, but standard methods typically break when the point of merger is overstepped. We present a rewrite of Peters' equations in $\ln$-space, which allows common numerical solvers to converge. This leads to a more numerically robust and computationally efficient method for evolving compact binaries due to gravitational wave emission, reducing the number of function evaluations by 60\% to 70\% in our tests.
