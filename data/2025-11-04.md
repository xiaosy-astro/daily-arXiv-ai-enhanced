### [1] [Shooting for the stars: Jet-mode feedback and AGN jet deceleration from stellar mass-loading](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.26881)
*Talia M. O'Shea,Sebastian Heinz,Melinda Soares-Furtado,Zsofi Igo,Andrea Merloni*

Main category: astro-ph.HE

TL;DR: 该论文提出了一个恒星质量加载导致AGN喷流减速的简单模型，研究局部宇宙中直接喷流反馈的能量预算。研究发现约25%的喷流功率在星系内被有效减速并可用于直接反馈，且这一比例对低爱丁顿分数下的射电AGN发生率函数形状不敏感。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 研究AGN喷流如何通过动能反馈影响星系演化，特别是狭窄相对论性喷流如何与星系耦合这一开放性问题。喷流减速被认为是实现有效耦合的必要条件，通常归因于物质（如恒星风）的卷入。

Method: 建立恒星质量加载导致喷流减速的简单模型，包括推导主序星质量损失率随恒星群体年龄变化的经验公式，并将质量损失数据与射电AGN发生率的参数拟合相结合。

Result: 预测大多数喷流在其宿主星系内被减速，并基本复制了LERGs中预期的FR-II比例。计算显示在任何恒星群体年龄下，局部宇宙中约25%的喷流功率被有效减速并可用于直接反馈。恒星质量损失率随时间显著演化，近似遵循τ^{-1.1}关系。

Conclusion: 尽管渐近巨星支（AGB）恒星在所有年龄阶段都主导质量损失，但在低质量星系中它们的随机性很重要。研究还推导了一个临界喷流功率，低于该功率时仅主序星就足以使喷流减速。

Abstract: AGN jets are thought to be vital ingredients in galaxy evolution through the
action of kinetic feedback; however, how narrow, relativistic outflows couple
to galaxies remains an open question. Jet deceleration, which is often
attributed to the entrainment of material, such as stellar winds, is thought to
be necessary for efficient coupling. We present a simple model of jet
deceleration due to stellar mass-loading to investigate the energy budget of
direct jet feedback in the local Universe. To this end, we produce models of
stellar mass-loss, including deriving a prescription for main sequence
mass-loss rates as a function of stellar population age. We pair this mass-loss
data with a parametric fit for radio AGN incidence, predicting that a majority
of jets are decelerated within their hosts, and generally replicate the
expected FR-II fraction in LERGs. We calculate that $\gtrsim$25\% of the jet
power in the local Universe is efficiently decelerated and available for direct
feedback within galaxies for any stellar population age. This fraction is
largely invariant to the shape of the radio AGN incidence function at low jet
Eddington fractions. The stellar mass-loss rate evolves significantly over
time, approximately following $\tau^{-1.1}$, leading to corresponding decreases
in decelerated jet power in older stellar populations. Although asymptotic
giant branch (AGB) stars dominate mass-loss at all ages, we find that their
stochasticity is important in low-mass galaxies, and derive a critical jet
power below which main sequence stars alone are sufficient to decelerate the
jet.
